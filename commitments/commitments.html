What is coeliac disease? A global perspective

Coeliac disease is not just an allergy, it is a serious autoimmune condition that affects millions worldwide. When individuals with coeliac disease consume gluten, it triggers an immune response that can damage the small intestine, leading to long-term health complications.

  • Global Impact: An estimated 1 in 100 people worldwide have coeliac disease, but up to 70% of cases remain undiagnosed.
  • Risks of Being Undiagnosed: Untreated coeliac disease can lead to malnutrition, osteoporosis, infertility, and even certain types of cancer.
  • A Surprising Fact: Many people are misdiagnosed or remain unaware of their condition for years. Early detection and dietary changes are crucial for long-term health.
